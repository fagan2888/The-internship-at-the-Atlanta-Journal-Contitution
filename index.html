Internships at The Atlanta Journal-Consitution

About the Internship

The AJC does fall, spring and summer internships. Fall/spring semester internships offer local students to earn college credit and/or pay. Summer internships are paid, 10-week programs. The AJC is looking for upper-level college students interested in pursuing a career as a reporter, database specialist or interactive producer. Interns are treated as full-time professional journalists People who have held internship positions are frequently considered first choice options when positions open up.

Internship Requirements

Interns must be rising junior, senior or graduate level students. Those who have graduated within six months of the start of the internship are also eligible Database specialist or interactive producer candidates must also have the following skills: “Solid” web programming skills, experience with HTML5, CSS3, XML, JavaScript, jQuery and Ajax, kills making clean, clear, interactive graphics Application requirements: ◦A short note on why you want to be a journalist and how interning at the AJC will help you reach your goals ◦Specify the internship you are interested in: Reporting, interactive graphics or database work ◦3 links to or pdfs of your best work ◦Resume ◦References with contact information for two ◦*Database specialist and interactive producer candidates must also submit a link to an online portfolio Applications for spring/summer internships are due November 1, 2014. Applications for fall internships are closed.
